The AridGuard smart contract is the foundation of the parametric drought insurance system. It manages policy purchases, stores policy data on-chain, and executes automated payouts when triggered by the Climate Risk Engine (CRE).

## Contract Overview

The AridGuard contract is deployed on Base Sepolia and implements a trust-minimized insurance protocol where:

- Farmers purchase policies by paying premiums in ETH
- Policy data (location, premium) is stored immutably on-chain
- Only the authorized CRE address can trigger payouts
- Payouts are 10x the premium paid

## Architecture

```solidity
contract AridGuard is Ownable {
    address public creAddress;
    
    struct Policy {
        address farmer;
        int256 lat;
        int256 long;
        uint256 premiumPaid;
        bool isActive;
    }
    
    mapping(bytes32 => Policy) public policies;
    bytes32[] public activePolicyIds;
}
```

### Key Components

<ParamField path="creAddress" type="address">
  The authorized Climate Risk Engine address that can execute payouts
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="policies" type="mapping(bytes32 => Policy)">
  Mapping of policy IDs to Policy structs containing farmer details and coverage information
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="activePolicyIds" type="bytes32[]">
  Array of all active policy IDs for enumeration
</ParamField>

## Policy Structure

Each policy contains the following fields:

<ResponseField name="farmer" type="address">
  The wallet address of the farmer who purchased the policy
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="lat" type="int256">
  Latitude coordinate of the insured location (scaled integer)
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="long" type="int256">
  Longitude coordinate of the insured location (scaled integer)
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="premiumPaid" type="uint256">
  The amount of ETH paid as premium (in wei)
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="isActive" type="bool">
  Whether the policy is currently active and eligible for payout
</ResponseField>

## Events

The contract emits two primary events:

### PolicyPurchased

```solidity
event PolicyPurchased(
    bytes32 indexed policyId,
    address indexed farmer,
    int256 lat,
    int256 long,
    uint256 premium
);
```

Emitted when a farmer purchases a new insurance policy.

<ResponseField name="policyId" type="bytes32" required>
  Unique identifier for the policy
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="farmer" type="address" required>
  Address of the farmer who purchased the policy
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="lat" type="int256" required>
  Latitude of the insured location
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="long" type="int256" required>
  Longitude of the insured location
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="premium" type="uint256" required>
  Premium amount paid in wei
</ResponseField>

### PayoutExecuted

```solidity
event PayoutExecuted(
    bytes32 indexed policyId,
    address indexed farmer,
    uint256 payoutAmount
);
```

Emitted when the CRE executes a payout for a policy.

<ResponseField name="policyId" type="bytes32" required>
  ID of the policy receiving the payout
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="farmer" type="address" required>
  Address receiving the payout
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="payoutAmount" type="uint256" required>
  Amount paid out in wei (10x premium)
</ResponseField>

## Constructor

```solidity
constructor(address _creAddress) Ownable(msg.sender)
```

<ParamField path="_creAddress" type="address" required>
  Initial address authorized to execute payouts (Climate Risk Engine)
</ParamField>

The constructor sets the initial CRE address and establishes the deployer as the contract owner.

## Funding the Contract

The contract includes a `receive()` function to accept ETH deposits:

```solidity
receive() external payable {}
```

This allows the contract to be funded for payouts. The contract must maintain sufficient balance to cover potential 10x payouts.
